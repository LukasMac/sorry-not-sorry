Rules

1. Setup

Shuffle the deck and deal 7 cards to each player. Place the remaining cards face down as the draw pile.

2. Taking Turns

On your turn, draw a card and read the situation out loud. Choose another player who must respond with either a "Sorry" or "Not Sorry" answer.

3. Scoring

If other players vote that your response was genuine and fitting, you keep the card as a point. If not, the card goes to the discard pile.

4. Winning

The first player to collect 5 cards wins! They are crowned the Sorry Not Sorry champion of brutal honesty.

Setup: The game is for 2-10 players, ages 7 and over. Every player starts with seven cards, and they are dealt face down. The rest of the cards are placed in a Draw Pile face down. Next to the pile a space should be designated for a Discard Pile. The top card should be placed in the Discard Pile, and the game begins!

Card deck is composed of these cards:

Regular cards:

All regular cards have a number from 0 to 10 (excluding 9, because it can be easily confused with 6) and also a background color which can be one of four colors: amber, violet, turquoise, rouge.

  • There are 8 cards of each number (two for each color) except 0 number cards. There are only 4 cards with number 0 - one for each number.

Action Cards: Besides the number cards, there are several other cards that help mix up the game. These are called Action or Symbol cards.

  • U-turn – If going clockwise, switch to counterclockwise or vice versa. It can only be played on a card that matches by color, or on another Reverse card. If turned up at the beginning of play, the dealer goes first, and the player to the dealer’s right is next (normally it would be the player to the dealer’s left). There are in total 4 reverse cards, one for each color.
  • Sleep – When a player places this card, the next player has to skip their turn. It can only be played on a card that matches by color, or on another Skip card. If turned up at the beginning of play, the first player (to the dealer’s left) loses his/her turn. The next player to that player’s right starts the game instead. There are in total 4 skip cards, one for each color.
  • Draw Two – When a person places this card, the next player will have to pick up two cards and forfeit his/her turn. It can only be played on a card that matches by color, or on another Draw Two. If turned up at the beginning of play, the first player draws two cards and gets skipped. There are in total 8 skip cards, two for each color.
  • Color change – This card represents all four colors, and can be placed on any card. The player has to state which color it will represent for the next player. It can be played regardless of whether another card is available. If turned up at the beginning of play, the first player chooses what color to continue play. This card cancels silent mode and players can start talking again. There are in total 2 Color change cards.
  • Color change shhh mode – This card represents all four colors, and can be placed on any card. The player has to state which color it will represent for the next player. It can be played regardless of whether another card is available. If turned up at the beginning of play, the first player chooses what color to continue play. This card also means that players can not talk anymore and if anyone says any work they take two cards as a penalty. There are in total 2 Color change shhh cards.
  • Color change Draw Four – This acts just like the Color change card except that the next player also has to draw four cards as well as forfeit his/her turn. With this card, you must have no other alternative cards to play that matches the color of the card previously played. If you play this card illegally, you may be challenged by the other player to show your hand to him/her. If guilty, you need to draw 4 cards. If not, the challenger needs to draw 6 cards instead. If turned up at the beginning of play, return this card to the Draw pile, shuffle, and turn up a new one. There are 4 Color change draw four cards in total.
  • Thief - There are 2 thief cards in the deck. Player who plays it can swap his/her hands with any player of his/her choice. This card does not have a color therefore the previous card decides the color.
  • Challenge player of your choice - Player who plays this card has to challenge any player of his/her choice. The challenge is simple, you both count to three and on number three with your finger you show either one or two fingers. If both of you show the same amount of fingers then the challenged player draws two cards otherwise the challenger draws two cards.
  • Number card 7 - once this card is played all players have to put their hand in the middle and the player who is the last one draws two cards as a penalty.

Game Play: The first player is normally the player to the left of the dealer (you can also choose the youngest player) and gameplay usually follows a clockwise direction. Every player views his/her cards and tries to match the card in the Discard Pile.

You have to match either by the number, color, or the symbol/Action. For instance, if the Discard Pile has a red card that is an 8 you have to place either a red card or a card with an 8 on it. You can also play a Color change card (which can alter current color in play).

If the player has no matches or they choose not to play any of their cards even though they might have a match, they must draw a card from the Draw pile. If that card can be played, play it. Otherwise, keep the card, and the game moves on to the next person in turn. You can also play a Color change card, or a Color change Draw Four card on your turn.

If a player before you plays “Color change draw four” or “draw two card” the next player either draws four or two cards respectively OR the player can pass it to the next player by playing draw four or draw two cards respectively.

If the very first card is an action card then put it back to the middle of the draw pile and take another card on the top until it’s not an action card anymore.

At any time during the game, if the Draw Pile becomes depleted and no one has yet won the round, take the Discard Pile, shuffle it, and turn it over to regenerate a new Draw Pile.

Generally making any type of mistake during game play means that player who made a mistake must draw two cards as a penalty.

About

Sorry Not Sorry is a playful card game where players navigate through awkward social situations with a mix of honesty and humor. Created for those who love to laugh at life's uncomfortable moments!

🎮 Fun First

Designed to create memorable moments and lots of laughter with friends and family.

🤝 Social Bonds

Simple rules, endless possibilities. Perfect for 3-8 players, ages 16+.

FAQ

How many players do I need?

The game works best with 3-8 players. More players = more fun!

How long does a game take?

A typical game lasts 30-45 minutes, but you can play as long as you're having fun!

Is this game family-friendly?

The game is recommended for ages 16+ due to some mature themes and situations.

Can I add my own cards?

Absolutely! Creating your own cards adds a personal touch to the game.
