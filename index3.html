Rules

Setup of the game

  • Perfect for 2 to 10 players, but if you've got more friends than that, you might need to start a waiting list or consider if they're really all your friends.
  • Age recommendation: 7 and up, because anyone younger might try to eat the cards.
  • Each player gets dealt seven cards, placed in their hands face down (so you can't peek)
  • The rest of the deck? That's your Pick Pile placed face down.
  • Next to Pick Pile, designate a space for the Gameplay Pile.
  • Flip over the top card from the Pick Pile and place the first card to the Gameplay Pile face up to kick things off. Make sure that the first card is not an action card. If it is an action card, place it in the middle of the Pick Pile and repeat the process until you find a non action card.
  • And voila! You're now ready to unleash the chaos of SorryNotSorry game playing. Let the fun begin!

Goal of the game

Be the first player without cards.

Core Game Play rules

The first player is normally the player to the left of the dealer or previous game winner. Gameplay starts in a clockwise direction. Player who has the turn tries to match the card in the Gameplay Pile except in cases when the player does cut in move (see special game rules).

Matching the card

Players have to match card either by number, color or action. For example if the top Gameplay Pile card:

  • is a violet with number 4, then other players have to place either any violet card or any card with number 4.
  • is a turquoise U-turn, then other players have to place either any turquoise card or any color U-turn card.

Exceptions to this rule

  • Colour Change Silent/Talk Mode
  • Color Change Extra 4 Cards
  • Thief Card

If the player has no matches or chooses not to play any of their cards even though they might have a match, they must draw one card from the Pick pile. If that card can be played you are free to play it or otherwise you can keep it to yourself and the game moves on to the next person in turn.

At any time during the game, if the Pick Pile becomes depleted and no one has yet won the round, take the Gameplay Pile, place the top card in the center then shuffle the rest of the cards and place them face down. This is your new Pick Pile.

Action cards

Besides the number cards, there are action cards to spice the game up.

U-turn

Reverses the direction of play (clockwise to counterclockwise or vice versa).

  • Can be played on a card of the same color or another U-Turn card.
  • Total: 8 cards (2 per color).

Sleep (Zzz...)

Skips the next player's turn.

  • Can be played on a card of the same color or another Sleep card.
  • Total: 8 cards (2 per color) in the deck.

Pick Two (+2)

The next player draws two cards and skips their turn.

  • Can be played on a card of the same color or another Pick Two card.
  • Total: 8 cards (2 per color) in the deck.

Change Color Pick Four (+4)

Lets the player choose the color for the next turn. The next player must draw 4 cards and skip their turn unless they play a Color Change Draw Four card and pass it on to the next player which now has to pick 8 cards and so on.

  • Can be played on any card.
  • Total: 4 cards in the deck.

Change Color Silent Mode

Lets the player choose the color for the next turn and starts Silent Mode.

  • In Silent Mode, players can only make sounds or laugh; anyone who speaks must draw 2 penalty cards.
  • Can be played on any card.
  • Identified by a zipped mouth.
  • Total: 2 cards in the deck.

Change Color Talk Mode

Lets the player choose the color for the next turn and cancels Silent Mode (if active).

  • Can be played on any card.
  • Identified by an open mouth.
  • Total: 2 cards in the deck.

Thief

Allows the player to swap hands with any other player.

  • This card has no specific color, number, or action, so the previous card determines the next action.
  • Total: 2 cards in the deck.

Number 7

While not technically an action card, it triggers a reaction:

  • All players must place their hands in the center. The last player to do so draws 2 cards as a penalty.

Special game play rules to make game even more fun

Yell Sorry / Not Sorry - The moment a player has just one card left in their hands they must yell “Sorry”. If they are caught not saying “Sorry” by another player before the next player has taken their turn, that player must draw two new cards as a penalty. Assuming that the player is unable to play/discard their last card and needs to draw, but after drawing, is then able to play/discard that card, the player has to repeat the action of calling out “SORRY”. The bottom line is – Announcing “SORRY” needs to be repeated every time you are left with one card. If any player says “NOT SORRY” before the player who has one card left trying to counter them then the player with one card has to draw two cards as a penalty. Generally there can be three cases when it comes to two players saying “SORRY/NOT SORRY”:
  • When a player who has one card left is clearly the first to yell “SORRY”. Then the player who tried to counter has to draw two cards as a penalty.
  • When both players start yelling “SORRY/NOT SORRY” almost at the same time (or when it is hard to tell who was the first), then both players take one card each as a penalty
  • When a player who has one card left is clearly not first to say “Sorry”, then the player who has one card left draws two cards as a penalty.
If more than two players try to say “SORRY/NOT SORRY” the same rules apply to all two players.
Stacking cards -

during your turn you can stack multiple cards as long as they are the same number. For example if you have two cards with number 8 (one rouge and one violet) you can play both cards at once, but make sure you match the card on top of the Gameplay Pile. Players can stack action cards as well. Stacking Change Color Pick Four or Pick Two cards mean that the next player must draw the total sum of all stacked cards. For example, playing three stacked Pick Two cards means that the next player must draw 6 cards in total unless they play “Pick two” card themselves which means that the next player must draw 8 cards and so on. Stacking U-turn cards basically means if the total amount of reverse cards is odd then direction is reversed otherwise the playing direction is the same.

There is one card that players can not stack - it's the Thief card. Players can only play one swap hands card at a time so don't collect them because it works against players advantage.

Cutting in -

cutting in means that a player can play without his/her turn. The condition to cut in is to have exactly the same card (matching number/action and matching color). Player has to be quicker to cut in before the player whose turn it normally is. If the player who is cutting in is late and places the card after the player whose turn it was to play next then the player who tried to cut it draws two cards as a penalty. If cut in is successful then the game continues from the player who did cut in. Players also can cut in with special cards.

Finishing a game without saying “Sorry” -

there is a case when a player can finish the game without yelling “Sorry” - by placing two exactly the same number and same color cards (pay attention it's only number and not action).

Silent mode -

when “Change Color Silent Mode” card is played it means that everyone in the game can not talk. Players can make sounds and laugh, but they can not say any word with the only exception being the word “Sorry/Not Sorry” when having one card left on your hands or when trying to counter another player. Silent mode is canceled once the “Change Color Talk Mode” card is played. During silent mode when any of “Change Color” cards is played, players point to the color on the card they want the next card to be.

Naming color when playing “Change Color” cards

when playing any of “Change Color” cards players must choose which color should the next card be. They MUST use one of these color names: rouge, amber, turquoise or violet. If the player says for example “red” instead of rouge then the player picks two cards as a penalty.

Winning the game
  • The last card can not be an action card, it must be a number card.
  • Players can not stack last cards unless they are number cards and have exactly the same color and the same number.
  • If the last card is the number which requires all players to place a hand in the middle then all players must do it and the last one takes two cards as a penalty which means that the player who was playing last card might get a penalty and then the game continues.

Extra spicy mods to the game to make it even more fun

  • Rolling hand placing number - instead of number 7 requiring placing hands in the middle, increase the number by 1 every time hand placing has happened. For example, once you play the number 7 card, the next card to place hands in the middle is number 8 card and so on (the next card after number 10 is number 0 card).
  • No mercy mode - if a player is not following the game and makes a mistake they must pick two cards as a penalty. Some examples of such cases:
    • does not play when it's their turn
    • forgets what was the color requested for Change Colour card
    • plays when it's not their turn
    • plays incorrect card

About

"Sorry Not Sorry" is a fast-paced, energetic, and hilariously chaotic card game that thrives on playful twists, unexpected turns, and ruining your friendships. With mischievous characters, vibrant designs, and clever action cards like "Skip," "Pick Four," and "Thief" every round keeps players on their toes. Whether you're sabotaging friends or trying to dodge their schemes, this game guarantees endless fun and laughter. Perfect for parties, game nights, or anytime you want to unleash your competitive (and cheeky) side!

🎮 Fun First

Simple rules, endless possibilities. Perfect for 3-8 players, ages 7+.

FAQ

How many players do I need?

The game works best with 3-8 players. More players = more fun!

What is the age range for "Sorry Not Sorry"?

The game is recommended for ages 7 and up, ensuring fun for kids and adults alike. Younger kids might find the rules tricky (or try to eat the cards).

What happens if the Pick Pile runs out of cards?

Simply shuffle the Gameplay Pile (except for the top card), and it becomes your new Pick Pile. The chaos continues uninterrupted!

Can action cards be used as the final card to win?

No, the last card must be a number card. Action cards can not be used to end the game.

What is the "Cutting In" action, and how does it work?

"Cutting In" allows a player to play out of turn if they have exactly the same card (matching number, color, and action) as the one currently on the Gameplay Pile. To successfully cut in, you must place your card before the next player takes their turn. If you're late or incorrect, you draw two cards as a penalty. A successful cut shifts the turn order to the cutting player, adding an exciting, unpredictable twist!

What makes this game different from other card games?

Unique mechanics like Silent Mode, Cutting In, and the hilarious Sorry/Not Sorry rule create a fast-paced, unpredictable, and laugh-filled experience.

Can players stack action cards?

Yes! Stacking is allowed for matching action cards (like Pick Two or U-turn), increasing their effect. The exception is the Thief card, which must be played individually.

What happens in Silent Mode?

Players can’t talk but can laugh or make noises. Anyone caught speaking a word (other than "Sorry/Not Sorry") must draw two cards as a penalty.

Can we modify the rules?

Absolutely! The game includes fun extra mods, like Rolling Hand Placing Number or No Mercy Mode, but feel free to invent your own twists.

How long does a typical game last?

Depending on the number of players, a game can last 10-20 minutes, making it perfect for quick matches or longer gaming sessions.

Is "Sorry Not Sorry" suitable for family game nights?

Definitely! Its mix of strategy, humor, and fast-paced action makes it a hit for families, friends, or parties.

What if someone forgets to yell "Sorry" or "Not Sorry"?

The player faces penalties like drawing two extra cards, so be quick and sharp!

How do we decide which color to play after a "Change Color" card?

The player who plays the card must announce the new color (using names like rouge, amber, turquoise, or violet). Misnaming the color results in a penalty.

Can I play without action cards?

Yes! You can customize your deck to exclude or include specific cards based on the group's preference.
